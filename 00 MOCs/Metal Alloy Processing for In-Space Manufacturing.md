# Metal Alloy Processing for In-Space Manufacturing

## Research Focus

This PhD investigates how metal alloys solidify when buoyancy-driven transport is suppressed or strongly reduced, and how that behaviour can inform reliable in-space manufacturing of metallic parts.

The working thesis is that microgravity does not simply create a flow-free melt. It suppresses buoyancy and sedimentation, but exposes residual mechanisms such as Marangoni convection, shrinkage feeding, electromagnetic stirring, surface-pore-driven flow, g-jitter, recoil effects and melt-bridge instabilities. The useful research target is therefore to identify which mechanisms dominate under specific alloy/process conditions and convert that into process maps for in-space manufacturing.

## Current Research Pillars

1. **Gravity-sensitive solidification physics**: dendritic growth, CET, macrosegregation, undercooling, phase selection and shrinkage flow.
2. **Microgravity benchmark experiments**: ISS MSL/CETSOL/MICAST, sounding rockets, parabolic flights, drop tubes, ISS-EML/ELF and China Space Station levitation.
3. **Alloy families**: Al-Cu, Al-Si, Al-Ni, Ti alloys, Ni superalloys, refractory eutectics and metallic glasses.
4. **In-space manufacturing routes**: wire-fed laser deposition, powder-based AM, directed energy deposition, remelting, casting and liquid-solid mixture processing.
5. **Model-to-process translation**: converting Bridgman/levitation variables such as G, R, cooling rate and undercooling into AM variables such as power, scan speed, feed rate, melt-pool lifetime and bead geometry.

## Immediate PhD Direction

The strongest contribution is likely a bridge between **microgravity solidification benchmarks** and **AM-relevant melt-pool solidification**. Directional solidification and levitation experiments provide clean data for model validation, but in-space metal AM introduces free surfaces, steep gradients, rapid solidification, repeated remelting and constrained spacecraft atmospheres. That gap is currently more defensible than a generic review of "microgravity improves materials."

Near-term target:

- Build a structured evidence matrix from the existing paper notes.
- Choose an anchor alloy pair, preferably Al-Cu/Al-Si for evidence depth or Ti64/stainless steel for manufacturing relevance.
- Map mechanism dominance using Rayleigh/Grashof, Marangoni, Peclet, Stefan, capillary and Bond numbers.
- Draft a publishable review section titled **From diffusion-controlled benchmarks to in-space melt pools**.
