## One-Paragraph Overview

This paper investigates **dendritic growth, convection, eutectic solidification, thermophysical properties, macrosegregation** in **20wt% Sn, 27wt% Al, 4.0wt% Cu, 4.5 wt.% Cu** under **microgravity (µg)** conditions using **Bridgman, DECLIC-DSI, ISS**. It is primarily relevant to Flemings *Polyphase Alloys – Castings and Ingots; Fluid Flow; Plane Front Solidification (Polyphase/Eutectic)* and references models including Phase-field simulation, Mullins-Sekerka (stability), Flemings (mushy zone).

---

## Top Findings

1. These findings contribute to a deeper understanding of dendritic growth dynamics and offer valuable benchmark data that could aid in refining predictive models and improving control of dendritic microstructures in metallurgical applications.
2. The first set of experiments (DSI campaign) was conducted in 2010-2011, focusing primarily on cellular growth using a sample of Succinonitrile (SCN)-0.24 wt% camphor, which resulted in novel observations and enhancements to the numerical models of solidification [39, 60, 85-99].
3. In order to provide benchmark data for the simulations, the main objective of this work is to determine the stability band of dendritic patterns for a large set of pulling velocities V .
4. In fact, no elimination instability nor tertiary branching was observed during p long experiments with a single constant pulling velocity after the establishment of steady state, outside of sub-grain boundaries.
