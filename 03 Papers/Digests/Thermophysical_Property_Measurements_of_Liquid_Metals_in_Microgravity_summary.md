## Top Findings

1. In such conditions, the positioning forces are reduced by at least a factor of 100, and the sample is essentially force-free during the cooling phase (The fields required for inductive heating are of course the same in lg and in pg).
2. Deviations from rotational symmetry due to non-symmetric surface oscillations (which are used for the surface tension measurements) have a sufficiently high frequency so that their influence can be suppressed by temporal averaging.
3. For the measurement of thermophysical properties of liquid metals, the ESL and the EML are most suitable.
4. One of these projects, ThermoLab (Fecht, 1999) is devoted to the measurement of thermophysical properties of liquid metals with industrial relevance.
