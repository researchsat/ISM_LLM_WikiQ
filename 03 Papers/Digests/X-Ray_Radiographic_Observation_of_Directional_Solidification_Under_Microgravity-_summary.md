## Top Findings

1. It has been shown that microgravity (µg) environment is an efficient way to eliminate buoyancy and convection to provide benchmark data for the validation of models and numerical simulations [13-17].
2. Preliminary results show the high capabilities of this experimental set-up in terms of thermal behaviour, as well as X-ray observation.
3. The next step of the project is to perform an experiment in microgravity environment to obtain purely diffusive transport conditions and then to provide benchmark data for the validation of models and numerical simulations.
