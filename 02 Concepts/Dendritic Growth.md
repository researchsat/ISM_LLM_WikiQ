# Dendritic Growth

Dendritic growth is the branched growth morphology that develops when the solid-liquid interface becomes unstable. Dendrite tip velocity, tip radius, primary spacing, and secondary arm spacing encode the local thermal and solutal transport conditions.

## Gravity Dependence

Earth 1g convection changes solute transport around dendrite tips and arrays. Microgravity experiments are useful because they reduce buoyancy-driven advection, letting spacing-selection and dendrite-growth models be tested against a cleaner transport field.
