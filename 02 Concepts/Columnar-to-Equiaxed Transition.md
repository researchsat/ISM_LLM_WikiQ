# Columnar-to-Equiaxed Transition

The columnar-to-equiaxed transition is the shift from directionally aligned columnar dendrites to equiaxed grain growth. In alloy solidification, CET depends on constitutional undercooling, nucleation potency, grain refiners, thermal gradient, growth velocity, and transport of equiaxed grains.

## Gravity Dependence

In Earth 1g, CET is affected by both nucleation and transport. Buoyancy-driven flow can carry solute and equiaxed grains away from or toward the columnar front. In microgravity, buoyant grain transport is suppressed, so the nucleation distance and front-blocking process can be interpreted more cleanly.
