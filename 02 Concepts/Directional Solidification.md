# Directional Solidification

Directional solidification imposes a thermal gradient and growth direction so that the solid-liquid interface advances through the sample in a controlled way. It is the dominant experimental framework in the current reference folder for comparing 1g and microgravity solidification.

## Why It Matters

Directional solidification lets experiments control or estimate the temperature gradient `G`, growth velocity `V`, cooling rate, solute redistribution, and interface morphology. This makes it a strong framework for testing CET, dendrite spacing, and macrosegregation.
