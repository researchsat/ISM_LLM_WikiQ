# Microgravity as a Diffusion-Controlled Benchmark

Microgravity suppresses buoyancy-driven convection, allowing heat and solute transport to approach diffusion-controlled limits. This is why microgravity solidification experiments are valuable for validating phase-field, dendrite-growth, CET, spacing-selection, and macrosegregation models.

The phrase needs precision: microgravity is not zero-flow. Residual acceleration, g-jitter, shrinkage, Marangoni stress, electromagnetic fields, and wall effects can still drive flow.

## Use In Thesis

Use microgravity as the baseline for intrinsic solidification mechanisms. Use Earth 1g as the coupled transport case where convection and solid motion perturb that baseline.
