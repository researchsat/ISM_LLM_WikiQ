# Constitutional Undercooling

Constitutional undercooling occurs when solute rejected at the solid-liquid interface lowers the local liquidus temperature ahead of the interface enough that the liquid becomes unstable to cellular or dendritic growth.

## Gravity Dependence

On Earth, convection can thin, thicken, or distort the solute boundary layer. In microgravity, the solute field is closer to the diffusion length scale `D/V`, so morphological stability thresholds are more directly comparable with theory.
