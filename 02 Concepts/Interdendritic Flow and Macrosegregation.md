# Interdendritic Flow and Macrosegregation

Interdendritic flow is liquid movement through the mushy zone. Macrosegregation is the long-range composition variation that results when solute-rich and solute-poor liquid move relative to the solid skeleton.

## Driving Forces

- Buoyancy from thermal and solutal density gradients
- Solidification shrinkage and feeding
- Permeability gradients in the mushy zone
- Pressure gradients and wall effects
- Grain or dendrite fragment motion

## Gravity Dependence

In Earth 1g, buoyancy often dominates or masks other driving forces. In microgravity, buoyancy is suppressed, so shrinkage, wall heat transfer, and residual acceleration can be isolated more clearly.
