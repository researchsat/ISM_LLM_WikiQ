# Navier-Stokes With Buoyancy

In alloy solidification, buoyancy enters momentum transport through density changes caused by temperature and composition gradients. A common scaling writes the buoyancy contribution as proportional to `g(beta_T DeltaT + beta_C DeltaC)`.

## Why It Matters

Reducing `g` suppresses the buoyancy forcing that drives natural convection in Earth 1g solidification. This is the first-principles reason microgravity experiments can approach diffusion-controlled heat and solute transport.
