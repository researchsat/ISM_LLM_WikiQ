# Why Gravity Matters in Alloy Solidification

Gravity enters alloy solidification mainly through buoyancy-driven melt flow and solid-particle transport. Thermal and solutal gradients generate density gradients; detached grains, dendrite fragments, liquid droplets, and inclusions can sediment or float depending on their density relative to the melt.

In Earth 1g, this couples interface morphology, solute redistribution, macrosegregation, grain transport, and final microstructure. In microgravity, the buoyancy term is suppressed, so diffusion, capillarity, shrinkage, Marangoni flow, electromagnetic forcing, and residual acceleration become easier to isolate.
